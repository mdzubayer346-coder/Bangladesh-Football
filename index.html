Football Timeline

Decades of passion, struggle, and glory — the story of Bangladesh football.

1972
Foundation
BFF Established
Bangladesh Football Federation was founded just one year after independence, marking the official beginning of organized football in the nation.
1986

SAFF Championship
Bangladesh began competing on the South Asian stage, building rivalries and forging a national identity through football.

Golden Era

Bangladesh won their first and historic SAFF Championship, defeating rivals and etching a golden moment into national football history.

Our Story

The Bangladesh Dream

Bangladesh football was born in passion. From the narrow streets of Old Dhaka to the paddy fields of Sylhet, football has always been the language of the people — played barefoot, dreamed of deeply.

The Bangladesh Football Federation, established in 1972, laid the groundwork. Decades of hard work, regional competition, and growing domestic leagues have begun to form something real: a footballing identity.

Today, a new generation — some raised abroad, some from the heart of Bangladesh — is rewriting the story. Coached by international professionals, backed by passionate fans, and driven by relentless ambition, this squad wants more than SAFF glory. They want Asia.

"In ten years, Bangladesh football will not just be a regional story — it will be an Asian one. The talent is here. The hunger is real. The rise begins now." — Bangladesh Football Federation
